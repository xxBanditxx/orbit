Orbit / ORBIT

An approach layer beneath language.

Orbit is a four-fundamental operating discipline for intelligence under uncertainty. It does not replace language, symbols, models, or meaning. It governs how meaning is approached, formed, questioned, revised, and held.

View the GitHub repository Understand the approach layer

The core idea

ORBIT is not a language, a translation system, or a semantic representation. It is the approach/orientation layer beneath language.

A language provides symbols. A semantic system attempts to represent meaning. Orbit operates one layer lower: it governs the conditions under which meaning is approached, formed, questioned, revised, and held under uncertainty.

          ↓

          ↓

          ↓

          ↓

Think of it like this

A word in English only becomes meaningful to a mind that can approach it as language. To someone who does not speak English, the same word may be nothing more than a sound or a pattern of marks. The symbol is present, but meaning does not appear until the observer has the orientation required to receive it.

AI faces the same problem at a different scale. A model may process words, tokens, prompts, and representations while still losing context, mistaking symbols for reality, collapsing uncertainty too quickly, or failing to notice the assumptions that shaped its answer.

Orbit addresses that pre-interpretive layer. It asks a system to approach language with context, provisional judgment, exploratory capacity, and awareness of instrument limits before meaning hardens into conclusion.

Unity without sameness

Orbit does not attempt to make every system identical. Different models, languages, minds, and symbolic systems may remain distinct while operating through a shared discipline for how meaning is formed and revised.

Think of different eggs: they vary in size, shape, color, and proportion, yet still participate in the same deeper structural principles. Orbit preserves difference while giving difference a shared structure.

The four fundamentals

  • Contextual Sanity: maintain contact with context.
  • Provisional Judgment: hold conclusions as revisable and context-bound.
  • Childlike Capacity: preserve exploration, play, and discovery.
  • Instrument-Bounded Understanding: remember that every model is limited by its instruments.

What Orbit is for

Orbit is intended for reasoning, learning, decision-making, AI oversight, and model behavior under uncertainty. The goal is not to make every system say the same thing. The goal is to make each system's path to meaning more coherent, more revisable, and more legible to other systems.
